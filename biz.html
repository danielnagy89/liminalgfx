This was my first experience with working on a project... while it's LIVE! Handling the graphics optimization and environment lightmapping for 4 platforms simultaneously taught me a thing or two about dilligence and care when pushing to subversion. It was taxing but equally rewarding.

Alien vs Predator: Evolution (2013)

The first foray into a high profile project for a prestigious client- 20th Century Fox.

Fact Sheet

  • Developer: Angry Mob Games
  • Publisher: FOX Digital Entertainment
  • Release date: Q1, 2013
  • Platforms: Android, iOS, Ouya

Due to the heavy constraints of the mobile devices at the time, AVP Evolution was heavily reliant on trickery to shave off processing costs- lightmapping, occlusion culling and Light Probes were a blessing but also a constant breaking point. The way Unity calculated Occlusion Portals meant that I had to go in each level, test the camera extensively and rebake the Occlusion data and portals nearly daily, paying attention to the doors that might be open or occluding. I was in charge of meticulously checking the flags on all the props to make sure they receive proper lighting and don't pop in and out.

3D Video Game art was the path where I've sharpened my skills, deepened my work ethic and forged my versatility.

I've never been stuck on a career ladder, so I explored freely and brushed on just about every aspect of Game Art creation.
